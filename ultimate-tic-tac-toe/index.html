HOW TO PLAY

  • The big board is a 3 × 3 grid of small boards — 9 small boards in total.
  • X goes first. The first move can be any cell on any small board.
  • Where you play inside a small board decides which small board the next player must play in. Play the top-right cell → opponent goes to the top-right small board.
  • If that target small board is already won or full, the next player may play anywhere.
  • Win a small board with 3-in-a-row. It gets claimed with a big X or O.
  • Win the whole game by claiming 3 small boards in a row on the big grid.

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe — a bigger, brainier take on the classic

About this game

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe — also called meta tic-tac-toe or super tic-tac-toe — is a paper-and-pencil strategy game that turns the 30-second classic into something you can actually think about. Instead of one 3 × 3 grid, you play on nine of them, arranged into a larger 3 × 3 meta-grid. The twist: the cell you pick inside a small board forces your opponent to play in the small board that lives at that same position on the meta-grid. That single rule changes everything. Suddenly every move has two consequences — what it does to the small board you're playing in, and which board you're sending your opponent to. Good players learn to set traps: win a small board while also sending the opponent into a square where all their options are bad. It's free, it takes a minute to learn, and it can keep a family busy for a whole road trip.

Controls & tips
  • Tap an allowed cell to play. Active small boards glow; inactive boards are dimmed.
  • Undo (solo only) reverts both the computer's move and your last move.
  • New — restart with the same mode + difficulty. Menu — back to mode select.
  • Tip: the center small board is reachable from 8 other boards, so it gets contested fast.
  • Tip: playing a cell that would force the opponent to a won/drawn board gives them free choice — sometimes that's good (a draw destination), often it's risky.
